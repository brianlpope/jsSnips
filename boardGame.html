Given the following game rules and a game board consisting of an array of numbers greater or equal to zero, write code (C#, pseudo code, whatever) to determine if the board is solvable, in that, the end of the board is able to be reached.

Rules:

1. You start at the first position on the board. 2. From any given space on the board you may move forward any number of spaces up to the number indicated on your current space.

Example #1:

Board: [1,2,0,2]

From #0 you can move to #1 only. From #1, you can move either to #2 or #3. #2 is not a valid move, as you cannot move after that. Since you can move to #3, the board is solvable.

Example #2:

Board: [2,2,0,0,1]

From #0 you can move to #1 or #2 only. Since #2 is 0, and is not the end of the board, you cannot move past there, and as such, #2 is not a valid move. However, from #1, you can move either to #2 or #3, but since both #2 and #3 are zero, you cannot move forward past them, hence the board is not solvable.
